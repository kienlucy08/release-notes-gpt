**DevOps: Case Sensitivity and Collation Upgrade for PostgreSQL**
Area of Impact: System Hardening  
Strategic Theme: Platform Expansion  

Addressed the issue of case sensitivity for email fields in the database by upgrading PostgreSQL from version 17 to version 18 to restore case-insensitivity and improve text operations in the backend.
*Details:*  
- Case-insensitivity for email fields was initially managed through PostgreSQL collations.
- Recent table refactor led to the temporary disablement of collations for certain columns due to PostgreSQL 17 limitations.
- PostgreSQL 17 does not support pattern matching operations with non-deterministic collations, affecting filtering operations for partial string searches.
- Upgrading to PostgreSQL 18 resolves these limitations, allowing for the restoration of case-insensitivity.
*Impact to Users:*  
- Users can now enter email addresses with various casings on the login page without issues.
- Improves user experience by ensuring seamless login processes.
- Enhances backend text operations for email fields, reducing the risk of errors in manual processing.

*Note: This DevOps task focuses on backend software updates and may not be directly relevant for general users.*
